Unit 606 · 100 Ward St.

Contractor Elevator Use

Rules from HOA governing documents · Sourced from 3 PDFs
← Back to Reference Pages
Key rule in plain language: Contractors use the West elevator only, accessed from A Deck or B Deck (garage). Elevator pads are required every day. The main lobby elevator is off-limits. The homeowner is responsible for pad coordination through CWD.
Entry & Building Access Unit Remodel Policy (current) · Rule 17 & 2015 Rule 11
  • 17
    Entry — Current Policy
    All contractors, vendors, and persons working on the unit must enter and exit via A Deck or B Deck (garage entrances). Prohibited from using the building's main lobby entrance.
  • 11
    Entry — 2015 Policy (same intent)
    Workers enter and exit using only the A-Deck garage doorway. They will not use the building's front entry.
Elevator Use — Contractor Work Unit Remodel Policy (current) · Rule 18 & 2015 Rule 12
  • 18
    Which Elevator — Current Policy
    Materials and tools must be transported from A Deck or B Deck levels only, using the West elevator.
  • 18
    Elevator Pads — Required Every Day
    Elevator pads are required. The homeowner is responsible for coordinating installation of the pads through the CWD Move Coordinator. The carpet on the elevator floor will be protected by the hardboard insert.
  • 18
    End of Each Workday
    Unless repeating days of elevator padding are pre-arranged, at the end of each workday: elevator pads removed, ceiling panels re-installed, carpet cover removed.
  • 12
    Which Elevator — 2015 Policy (same)
    Materials and tools brought to unit from A-Deck only, using the West (left) elevator. Contractor coordinates pad installation with CWD Move Coordinator. Hardboard insert protects carpet.
Elevator Use — Moving Companies CWD Move Procedure Agreement · Rules 2–6
  • 2
    Initial Walkthrough
    An Association representative may walk with the moving company supervisor from the point of entry, to the elevator, and to the owner's condo. Any existing damage is noted on a checklist.
  • 3
    Elevator Key / Hold
    The elevator key allows the mover to hold the elevator at the entry floor and at the owner's floor to minimize move time. No furniture or boxes may be left in Common Elements. Prohibited from locking down or reserving the elevator if only one is operational.
  • 4
    Ceiling Panels
    Ceilings may not be removed from the elevator.
  • 5
    Elevator Dimensions
    It is the mover's responsibility to check actual dimensions of the elevator cabs before loading large items to prevent damage to the cab finish.
  • 6
    Protective Coverings — Required Before Move Starts
    In the absence of protective coverings, all items must be wrapped in moving blankets. No move can begin until protective coverings are in place.
Elevator Cleanup Unit Remodel Policy (current) · Rule 20 & 2015 Rule 14
  • 20
    Daily Cleanup — Owner's Responsibility
    The owner is responsible for ensuring all common areas are cleaned of debris or damage — including elevators, hallways, and garage decks. Cleanup should be done throughout the day. If not possible, by end of workday. Areas used by vendors must be sanitized daily.
